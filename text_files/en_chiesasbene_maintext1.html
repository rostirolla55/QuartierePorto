The Church of San Benedetto in Bologna, located in Via Indipendenza, has a long and complex history:

  • Origins: Its origins date back to the 13th century (some documents mention it as a parish as early as 1202). It was initially located just outside Bologna's second city walls.

  • 17th-century reconstruction: It was completely rebuilt in 1606 based on a design by Giovanni Battista Ballerini . At that time, it was officiated by the Minim Friars of San Francesco di Paola (known as "paolotti"), to whom it had been assigned in 1529.

  • Original Orientation: Until the end of the nineteenth century, the church overlooked Via Galliera , where the ancient façade (later redone in 1932) flanked by the bell tower is still visible today.

  • The opening of Via Indipendenza: The major transformation took place in 1892 with the opening of the new Via dell'Indipendenza . To adapt to the new layout and the new road, the orientation of the church was reversed : the apse became the new façade, which was rebuilt with the addition of a portico.

  • Interior: Despite the external transformations, the interior preserves important works by artists who were protagonists of the Bolognese Mannerism and Baroque periods, including paintings by Giacomo Cavedoni , Alessandro Tiarini and Lucio Massari .

In short, the current appearance of the church is the result of the seventeenth-century reconstruction and the radical reorientation at the end of the nineteenth century, when it was "turned" to face the modern Via Indipendenza.

Historical Sources for the Church of San Benedetto

 

  1. Sources of Toponymy and Urban History of Bologna:

    • Archives and Studies on the History of Via Indipendenza: These documents (often linked to the State Archives or the Archiginnasio Library) are crucial for precisely dating the great transformation of 1892 , the year in which the church was "turned" and the facade reoriented to face the newly created Via Indipendenza.

    • Studies on the Bolognese Churches: Academic texts and monographs which attest to their origins in the 13th century and the subsequent 17th century reconstruction (dated 1606 ), specifying the role of the architect Giovanni Battista Ballerini .

  2. Sources of the History of Art and Architecture of Bologna:

    • Art Guides and Catalogs: For a list of the interior works and artists. These sources confirm the presence of paintings by Baroque and Mannerist masters, such as Giacomo Cavedoni , Alessandro Tiarini , and Lucio Massari .

    • Texts on Religious Complexes: To identify the Order that managed it, namely the Minim Friars of San Francesco di Paola (the "Paolotti").

  3. Institutional Sites and Cultural Portals (Web):

    • Official Tourism and Culture Portals of the Municipality of Bologna (e.g. Bologna Welcome or History and Memory of Bologna ): These sites summarize and validate key historical data (13th century, 17th-century reconstruction, late 19th-century reorientation) for public use.

In summary, the information comes from a concordance of data present in historical archives, art history studies, and institutional sources that trace the evolution of this important Bolognese place of worship.
