The former tobacco factory on Via Riva di Reno in Bologna is a site of great historical and architectural interest, now enjoying a new lease of life linked to culture. Here's some key information, along with details about its history and current appearance:

History and Architecture

  • Address: Via Riva di Reno, 72, Bologna.

  • Origins: The Tobacco Factory was established at the beginning of the 19th century in the area that was the former convent of Santa Maria Nuova.

  • Renovation of 1906: The main building on Via Riva di Reno, where the canal once flowed (now covered in the center of the street), retains the historic fa√ßade which was completely redesigned in 1906 by the architect Gaetano De Napoli.

  • Art Nouveau: The facade is a notable example of Jugendstil (Art Nouveau), with a structure and decorations that reflect the taste of the time.

  • Workforce: Historically, the factory employed mostly women, who were considered more skilled than men at rolling tobacco leaves to make cigars.
