The Basilica of Santa Maria Maggiore , located in Via Galliera 10 , is the oldest church in Bologna dedicated to the Madonna and represents a treasure chest of history and art in the heart of the city.

History and Architecture

Its origins are ancient: tradition dates them to the 6th century (some sources even cite the 5th century), founded along the main artery of the Roman city. Over the centuries, it has undergone numerous transformations:

  • 12th century: Rebuilt and consecrated in 1187, it became the seat of a Collegiate Church of Canons.

  • 15th century (1464): It was enlarged with the addition of side chapels and the front portico.

  • 17th century (1665): It took on its current appearance thanks to the intervention of the architect Paolo Canali , who remodelled the central nave.

  • Modern era: The façade was completed in the upper part only in 1955. After the serious damage suffered in the 2012 earthquake, the church underwent a major restoration and reopened for worship in November 2019 .

Main Works of Art

The interior preserves masterpieces of the Bolognese school:

  • Altarpiece of the Assumption: Begun by Gio. Francesco Bezzi (il Nosadella ) and completed by Prospero Fontana (in the choir).

  • Paintings: Works by Alessandro Tiarini , Orazio Samacchini , Vincenzo Spisanelli , Mauro Gandolfi and Alessandro Guardassoni .

  • Fico Crucifix: An ancient wooden crucifix that tradition dates back to before the year 1000.

  • Decorations: The vault was decorated in the modern era (1936-1938) by the painter Eliseo Fumagalli, while the Chapel of the Holy Sacrament boasts precious stuccoes by Angelo Gabriello Piò .
