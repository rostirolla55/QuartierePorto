The work mentioned is located along the staircase leading to the upper rooms of the church and is not a painting by Annibale, as it is erroneously attributed to him, but by one of his successors from the Bolognese school.

  • Artist: Ludovico Mattioli (1662-1747)

  • Title (probable): Landscape with Saint Bartholomew

  • Location: Staircase of the Sanctuary of the Madonna della Pioggia and San Bartolomeo di Reno, Bologna.

  • Dating: 18th century.

Historical and Artistic Context:

  • Landscape in the 18th Century: Although Annibale Carracci pioneered the "ideal landscape" in the 17th century, Ludovico Mattioli worked in a later era, the 18th century, when the landscape genre was fully developed and independent. Mattioli, a Bolognese artist active between the late 17th and early 18th centuries, was known not only for his canvases but also as an engraver and landscape painter .

  • Style: His landscapes are typical of the late Baroque and Rococo style. Unlike Annibale's solemn and "heroic" nature, Mattioli's works present a more painterly , airy , and less structured style, with a predilection for atmosphere and light effects.

  • The Church: The Sanctuary of the Madonna della Pioggia (or San Bartolomeo di Reno) has undergone numerous renovations over the centuries. The current church is mostly from the 18th century (rebuilt according to a design attributed to Alfonso Torreggiani), and works such as that by Mattioli fit into this 18th-century decorative context.

  • Iconography: The inclusion of St. Bartholomew, the martyred apostle, serves as a reference to the ancient dedication of the church and the adjoining hospice.
