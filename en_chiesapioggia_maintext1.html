The Sanctuary of the Madonna della Pioggia, whose full name is Church of Santa Maria della Pioggia and San Bartolomeo di Reno, is located at the intersection of Via Galliera and Via Riva di Reno in Bologna.

Here is some key information:

  • Origin: It was originally known as the Church of San Bartolomeo di Reno and dates back to the 13th century as an Oratory.

  • Name: The name "Madonna della Pioggia" derives from an event in 1561, when a procession with the statue of the Madonna was associated with the end of a severe drought that had hit Bologna, bringing beneficial rains. Following this miraculous event, it was double-dedicated to the Saint and the Madonna.

It houses works of art by artists such as Agostino Carracci, Francesco Monti, Ludovico Mattioli (his fresco Landscape with Saint Bartholomew is located on the eighteenth-century staircase leading to the oratory) and a terracotta sculpture by Alfonso Lombardi (Saint Bartholomew).

  • Interior: The interior is It has a single nave with three side chapels and a frescoed barrel vault.

  • Statue: It houses the 13th-century statue of the Madonna della Provvidenza (also known as the Madonna della Pioggia), located to the left of the main altar and donated in 1435 by the Bolognese Senate.
