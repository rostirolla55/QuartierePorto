The former tobacco factory on Via Riva di Reno in Bologna is a place of great historical and architectural interest, with a new life linked to culture.

Here's the key information, along with details about its history and current appearance:

History and Architecture

Address: Via Riva di Reno, 72, Bologna.

Origins: The Tobacco Factory was established at the beginning of the 19th century in the area that was the former convent of Santa Maria Nuova.

Renovation of 1906: The main building on Via Riva di Reno, where the canal once flowed (now covered in the centre of the street), retains the historic fa√ßade which was completely redesigned in 1906 by the architect Gaetano De Napoli .

Art Nouveau: The facade is a notable example of Jugendstil (Art Nouveau), with a structure and decorations that reflect the taste of the time.

Workforce: Historically, the factory employed mostly women , who were considered more skilled than men at rolling tobacco leaves to make cigars.

Current Status and New Function

Today, the area of the former Manifattura is part of a major architectural and urban recovery project known as Manifattura delle Arti .

Cineteca di Bologna: The historic building on Via Riva di Reno is the current home of the Cineteca di Bologna , an internationally renowned cultural center for the preservation and promotion of cinema.

Gardens: Near the former Manifattura are also the Giardini ex Manifattura Tabacchi , a green area open to the public (generally from 7:00 am to 7:00 pm), often frequented by families and equipped with a play area for children.
